Postdocs: We are recruiting postdoctoral fellows who not only want to contribute to my vision, but also who want to build something. The advantages of being a new lab is that you will have direct, consistent mentorship from a physician-scientist who has navigated this career path recently and takes your independence seriously. I am committed to supporting K99 applications, first-author publications, and whatever comes next for you — whether that is a faculty position, industry, or something else. If you are drawn to functional readouts, mechanistic questions, and the challenge of joining a lab at its beginning, reach out.

Graduate Students: The Shah lab is affiliated with ___ and is actively recruiting graduate students for rotations. There are many opportunities to interact with other graduate stuents through our frequent co-meetings with ___ labs. Our work spans mouse genetics, live-cell imaging, mucociliary transport assays, ion transport physiology, bacterial killing and infection models and human airway models. A rotation here will give you direct exposure to techniques that most regeneration biology labs don't use and also not used by physiology labs. The central question — why regeneration restores cell identity but not tissue function — is tractable, important, and genuinely unsolved. If you are the kind of person who wants to understand _why_ something happens and not just _that_ it happens, this lab was built for you. Come do a rotation and see if it fits!

Research Technicians: We are looking for a research technician who wants to do real Science, not support it from a distance. You will work directly with the PI and other members on experiments from design to execution, learn techniques across mouse genetics, confocal imaging, and functional physiology assays, and be a genuine contributor to the lab's output. Co-authorship and even lead authorship is earned by contribution here, and I write strong letters for technicians who want to go on to graduate school, medical school, or professional programs. Prior experience in a research lab is helpful but less important than intellectual curiosity, technical rigor, and the desire to be here. If you want a position where your work matters from day one, get in touch.
